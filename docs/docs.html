View the flowchart below to help you visualize the process:

Question

  1. Identify your research topic:

    2. Start by selecting a specific research topic or subject area that interests you and has enough existing literature to warrant a meta-analysis or systematic review. Ensure the topic is relevant and significant within your field of study.

  2. Conduct a preliminary literature review:

    3. Before formulating your research question, conduct a preliminary literature review to identify existing studies and understand the current state of knowledge in the chosen area. Determine if there is a gap in the existing literature that warrants a meta-analysis or systematic review.

  3. PICO/PECO framework:

    4. Consider using the PICO (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome) or PECO (Population, Exposure, Comparison, Outcome) framework to structure your research question. This framework helps ensure that your question is specific and focused.

  4. Formulate a clear and concise research question:

    5. Ensure that your research question is clear, concise, and specific. It should be answerable through the synthesis of existing studies. Use precise language and avoid vague or overly broad questions. Make sure the question is phrased as a question and not a statement.
